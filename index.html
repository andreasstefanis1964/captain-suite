UNIQUE

EXPERIENCES in NEA SMYRNI

Midway between central Athens and the coast is the lush family-friendly neighbourhood of Nea Smyrni (tram from Syntagma to Aghia Fotinis, or an easy 25-minute walk). Its many squares are host to an interesting history: the neighbourhood gained its name from the former Greek city of Smyrna, now Izmir, from where refugees settled the area in the 1920s. Car-free Nea Smyrni Square is at the neighbourhood’s heart and is spectacular, with fountains, shops, restaurants, trendy cafes and bars to suit all ages, trends and budgets. With a plethora of outdoor entertainment during the summer, it’s a real find not on the typical tourist route. Venture beyond the square and you’ll find Nea Smyrni Alsos park, a large green oasis with aleppo pines, cypress and stone pines and good for strolling. It also has a jogging track, an outdoor gym and a cafe.

POPULAR

AREAS to visit in ATHENS

Athens, the capital of Greece, is a city rich in history, culture, and landmarks. Here are some popular areas to visit in Athens:

Acropolis of Athens

One of the most iconic landmarks in Greece

National Academy

One of the most prestigious academic institutions in Greece

The Odeon of Herodes Atticus

An ancient amphitheater located on the southwest slope of the Acropolis

Cultural events in the Odeon

It hosts a variety of concerts, theatrical productions, dance performances, and other artistic events throughout the year

The Entrance of "The Odeon"

The theater's dramatic setting against the backdrop of the Acropolis makes it a popular venue for both performers and audiences alike.

The National Gallery of Athens

Established in 1900 and features a diverse range of paintings, sculptures, prints, and other artistic media by Greek and international artists.

Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center (SNFCC)

Is a multifunctional complex that serves as a cultural, educational, and recreational hub for the city of Athens.

The Greek Parliament in Syntagma Square

The Evzones, or Presidential Guard, is a ceremonial unit responsible for guarding the Greek Parliament and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, located in front of the building

Monastiraki square and district

Is a bustling neighborhood known for its vibrant flea market, where you can find a variety of antiques, handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs

Ermou street "Shopping mile"

Is one of the main shopping streets in Athens running from Syntagma Square

Acropolis museun

Is one of the most important archaeological museums in Athens, Greece
